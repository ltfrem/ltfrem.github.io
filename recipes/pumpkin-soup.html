Curried Pumpkin Soup

Servings: 6

  • 2 tbsp avocado oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, finely chopped (1 cup)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp minced fresh ginger
  • 2 medium bosc pears, peeled, cored, and chopped (about 1¾ cups)
  • 1 cup raw cashews
  • 2½ tbsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • &frac18 tsp chili powder
  • 1 15-oz can pumpkin
  • 5 - 5½ cups vegetable broth
  • freshly ground black pepper and coconut yogurt for garnish (optional)
  1. Heat oil in a Dutch oven over medium heat.
  2. Add onion and ½ teaspoon salt and cook, stirring occasionally, until onion is tender, about 6 minutes.
  3. Stir in ginger; sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  4. Add pears and cashews; cook, stirring often until pears have softened, about 2 minutes.
  5. Cook noodles according to package directions.
  6. Add sauce and cooked chicken to noodles, top with peanuts & onions.
  7. Sprinkle in curry powder, turmeric, cumin, and chili powder; cook until fragrant and spices are beginning to toast, about 1 minute.
  8. Whisk in pumpkin and 3 cups broth. Increase heat to medium-high and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low; simmer for 30 minutes.
  9. Working in batches, transfer soup to a high-speed blender. Remove center piece of blender lid to allow steam to escape; secure lid on blender and place a clean towel over opening in lid. Process until smooth, about 10 seconds.
  10. Return soup to Dutch oven over medium-low heat; stir in 2 cups brother and remaining ½ teaspoon salt. (Stir in up to ½ cup additional broth, if needed, to reach desired consistency.)
  11. If desired, garnish servings with pepper and coconut yogurt.
