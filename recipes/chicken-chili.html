White Chicken Chili

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 2 (4.5oz) cans green chiles
  • 3 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into thirds
  • 5 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • salt & pepper
  • 2 (15oz.) cans white beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 ¼ cups frozen corn
  • ½ cup sour cream or plain greek yogurt
  • Freshly chopped cilantro, for garnish
  • ¼ cup shredded monterey jack cheese
  • ¼ cup crushed tortilla chips
  1. In a large pot, heat oil over medium heat. Add onion and jalapeño and cook until soft, about 5 minutes. Add garlic, oregano, and cumin and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add green chiles, chicken, and broth and season with salt and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer, covered, 10 - 12 minutes, until chicken is tender and cooked through.
  2. Transfer chicken to a plate and shred with two forks. Return to pot and add white beans and corn. Bring to a simmer and let cook, 10 minutes, using a wooden spoon to mash about ¼ of the beans. Turn off heat and stir in sour cream/yogurt.
  3. Ladle chili into bowls and garnish with cilantro, cheese, and chips before serving.
