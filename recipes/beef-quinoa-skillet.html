Texas Spicy Black Bean Chowder

Servings: 6

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 cup chopped onion
  • ¾ chopped red sweet pepper
  • 2 cups chopped round red potatoes
  • 1 15oz. can black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup frozen corn
  • 2 4oz. cans chopped green chilies
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 ½ teaspoon chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 cup half & half
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar or monterey jack cheese
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • chopped fresh tomato
  1. In a dutch ove, melt butter over medium heat. Add onion and sweet pepper; cook 5 minutes or until soft, stirring occasionally.
  2. Add potatoes, beans, corn, green chilies, broth, chili powder, and cumin. Bring to boiling, then reduce heat and simmer, covered for 10-15 minutes or until potatoes are tender.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together half & half and flour; add to soup. Cook and stir until slightly thickened and bubbly.
  4. Cook and stir 1 minute more. Stir in cheese until melted and smooth.
  5. Stir in fresh cilantro. Top with chopped fresh tomato.
  6. Sprinkle with chopped cilantro and chopped tomatoes and serve.
