Roasted Tomatillo Salsa

Makes 2 ¼ cups

  • 1 pound tomatillos, husked, thoroughly rinsed, and dried
  • 1 jalapeño pepper (2 for spicy salsa)
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, thick stems removed
  • ¼ cup finely chopped white onion
  • salt and pepper
  1. Set a rack in the highest part of the oven (about 4 inches from the broiler) and preheat the oven to broil. Line a rimmed baking sheet with aluminum foil.
  2. Place the tomatillos and jalapeño on the prepared baking sheet. Broil for 5-8 minutes, until the tops are charred. Flip the tomatillos and jalapeño and broil until their skins have black splotches and are blistered, 5-8 minutes more. Remove from the oven and let cool slightly.
  3. In a food processor or blender, combine the tomatillos and jalapeño, any accumulated juices from the baking sheet, the cilantro, and ¼ cup water. Pulse until pureed.
  4. Transfer to a bowl and add the onion, ½ teaspoon salt, and ⅛ teaspoon pepper. Let cool to room temperature and transfer to an airtight container. Store in the refrigerator for up to 1 week or in the freezer for up to 1 month.
