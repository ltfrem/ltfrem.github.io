VWhite Bean and Kale Quesadillas with Roasted Tomatillo Salsa

Makes 4 servings

  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 3 tablespoons minced shallot
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespon finely chopped fresh flat leaf parsley
  • 2 15oz. can2 cannelini beans, drained and rinsed (or 3 cups cooked cannelini beans)
  • salt and pepper
  • 5 cups roughly chopped lacinto (dinosaur or tuscan) kale, stems removed
  • 2-3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 10 8-10" flour tortillas
  • 2 ½ cups shredded monterey jack cheese
  • ¾ cups roasted tomatillo salsa
  1. Preheat oven to 250.
  2. In a medium nonstick skillet over medium heat, heat 1 tablespoon of oil until shimmering. Add the shallot and cook until translucent, 2-3 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute. Add the beans, parsley, ¼ teaspoon salt, and ⅛ teaspoon pepper and mix well. Cook until the beans are hot, about 3 minutes. Transfer to a bowl and mash the beans with a fork, leaving a few chunks for texture.
  3. Wipe out the skillet, returning to medium heat, and add the remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil. Once the oil is shimmering, add the kale and sauté until wilted, about 4 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Set aside.
  4. In a grill pan or a separate large skillet over medium heat, melt a small pat of butter. Place a tortilla in the pan and sprinkle 3 tablespoons of cheese over the entire tortilla. Cook until the cheese has melted, then distribute about 2 tablespoons of the white bean mixture and some of the kale over just half the tortilla. With a spatula, fold the tortilla in half to sandwich the filling. Sprinkle the folded tortilla with a pinch of salt. Cook until the bottom is golden brown on the second side, about 1 minute more. Transfer to a rimmed baking sheet and place in the oven to keep warm. Repeat with the remaining tortillas, cheese filling, and kale.
  5. Remove the quesadillas from the oven, cut each in half, and serve with salsa.
